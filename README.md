ONA Synthetic Benchmark — v3
An interactive, browser-native tool for generating and visualising synthetic Organisational Network Analysis (ONA) benchmark datasets.
Open `ona_benchmark_v3_enhanced.html` in any modern browser. No install, no server, no dependencies.

A Note on Realism and Applicability
The networks generated by this tool are synthetic by design. They are constructed from statistical models and calibrated parameters, not from observed relational data.
This means:
Topological plausibility, not empirical accuracy. The topology families (Erdős–Rényi, Watts–Strogatz, Barabási–Albert, SBM, Hierarchical Corporate) produce networks with properties that resemble real organisational networks in specific respects — clustering, hierarchy, hub formation — but any given generated network may not reflect how a real organisation of that size and industry actually operates.
Synthetic outcomes are benchmarks, not predictions. The `engagement_score`, `innovation_score`, `burnout_risk`, and `adoption_velocity` variables are derived from structural position using plausible but simplified assumptions. They are suitable for testing dashboard rendering, method validation, and algorithm benchmarking — not for drawing conclusions about real people or real organisations.
Industry templates are defaults, not ground truth. The department mixes, seniority distributions, and location weights in the four industry templates are reasonable approximations, not validated empirical distributions.
Scenario realism depends on parameter calibration. The scenarios are tuned to produce structurally interesting networks for specific business questions. Whether any given scenario accurately represents the dynamics of a real reorganisation, merger, or AI adoption programme is a matter of expert judgement, not a guarantee of the tool.
In short: these datasets are well-suited for method development, tool testing, training exercises, and demonstrating ONA concepts — and should be treated with appropriate caution if used to inform decisions about real organisations.
This note reflects the protocol author's own framing of the boundaries of v3, as stated in the original specification:
> *"v3 is a major expansion of the benchmark protocol, but it is still a benchmark system rather than a complete digital twin of organisational life. [...] synthetic outcomes are designed for benchmarking, not causal inference."*
> — Fierăscu, S.I., ONA Synthetic Benchmark Protocol v3, Limitations

What Is ONA?
Organisational Network Analysis maps how work actually happens inside a company — who collaborates, who seeks advice, who trusts whom, where silos form, where brokers carry too much load, and how change moves through the organisation. Unlike org-chart analysis, ONA works on the informal relational structure that drives productivity, innovation, and resilience.
Synthetic benchmark datasets allow researchers, practitioners, and product teams to:
test and compare ONA algorithms on controlled, reproducible ground truth,
demonstrate ONA tooling without using real employee data (privacy-safe),
model specific business questions before collecting real survey data,
validate platform outputs against known structural properties.

Quick Start
Download `ona_benchmark_v3_enhanced.html`
Open it in Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge
Choose a scenario from the left panel
Adjust node count, seeds, and active layers as needed
Click Generate
No internet connection is required after the file is saved locally.

Network Topologies
Five topology families are available, covering the standard baseline suite:
ID	Model	Real-world analogy
`er`	Erdős–Rényi	Fully random baseline
`ws`	Watts–Strogatz	Clustered teams with shortcut bridges
`ba`	Barabási–Albert	Hub-heavy influence concentration
`sbm`	Stochastic Block Model	Department-based clustering and silos
`hier_corp`	Hierarchical Corporate	Formal hierarchy plus informal overlays
Scenarios can combine a primary topology with overlays (e.g. `hier_corp` + `sbm` layers department clustering on top of a management hierarchy).
---
Relational Layers
Each network can include up to 10 relational layers. Directed layers model asymmetric relationships such as advice-seeking or reporting lines.
Layer	Type	What it captures
`communication`	Undirected	Day-to-day information exchange
`trust`	Undirected	Psychological safety and reliance
`collaboration`	Undirected	Joint work on tasks and projects
`innovation`	Undirected	Idea-sharing and creative exchange
`energy`	Undirected	Who energises or drains whom
`advice`	Directed	Who seeks expertise from whom
`reporting`	Directed	Formal management line
`mentorship`	Directed	Developmental guidance relationships
`decision_influence`	Directed	Who shapes decisions
`tool_interaction`	Directed	Human-to-AI-agent workflows
Edge weights are context-aware: same-department, same-location, and same-legacy-company pairs receive calibrated weight adjustments per layer.

Scenario Catalog
Ten benchmark scenarios are included, each targeting a specific business question. All are tuned for organisations of 500–3,000 employees.
DEMO_M — Executive Demo
Catalog ID: `ONA-BMK-DEMO-M-v1` · Default size: 120 nodes · Topology: `hier_corp` + `sbm`
A mid-size tech company with visible departmental silos, broker fragility, and a clear management hierarchy. Designed for executive presentations and tool demonstrations. Privacy-safe by construction.
---
CHRO_M — HR Analytics and Workforce Intelligence
Catalog ID: `ONA-BMK-CHRO-M-v1` · Default size: 500 nodes · Topology: `sbm`
A professional services firm with multi-site fragmentation and location homophily. Designed for HR analytics use cases: attrition risk, flight-risk identification, and cross-site collaboration gaps.
---
REORG_M — Reorganisation and Team Restructuring
Catalog ID: `ONA-BMK-REORG-M-v1` · Default size: 800 nodes · Topology: `hier_corp` + `sbm`
Simulates a corporate reorganisation with team merges and leadership succession scenarios. Supports what-if simulation and org design analysis. Temporal snapshots: pre- and post-reorg.
---
RESEARCH_SM — Research Benchmark Corpus
Catalog ID: `ONA-BMK-RESEARCH-SM-v1` · Default size: 100–500 nodes · Topology: corpus (all 5 families)
A full comparative corpus — one dataset per topology family at small and medium scale — with a shared attribute seed. Intended for method standardisation, algorithm benchmarking, and academic reproducibility.
---
DEV_S — Developer and API Testing Pack
Catalog ID: `ONA-BMK-DEV-S-v1` · Default size: 50–500 nodes · Topology: multi-pack
A developer-oriented pack covering all topology families, multiplex configurations, and temporal variants. Useful for API integration testing and UI development.
---
AI_M — AI Adoption and Human-Agent Workflows
Catalog ID: `ONA-BMK-AI-M-v1` · Default size: 600 nodes · Topology: `hier_corp` + `sbm` + AI agents
Models an organisation mid-AI-adoption with non-human actor nodes (AI agents), tool-interaction directed edges, adoption champion seeding, and change-readiness attributes. Temporal snapshots: Q1–Q4. Includes an additional outcome variable: `adoption_velocity`.
---
RESIZE_M — Restructuring, Rightsizing, and Delayering
Catalog ID: `ONA-BMK-RESIZE-M-v1` · Default size: 1,000 nodes · Topology: `hier_corp` + `sbm`
A manufacturing company undergoing workforce reduction. Models manager overload, span-of-control stress, and post-change articulation point risk. Snapshots: baseline and post-change.
---
MA_M — Post-Merger Integration
Catalog ID: `ONA-BMK-MA-M-v1` · Default size: 1,500 nodes · Topology: `sbm_dual_legacy`
Two legacy organisations merged into one. Nodes carry a `legacy_company` attribute; cross-legacy tie formation is suppressed at baseline and grows through snapshots. Models trust lag, culture integration friction, and talent retention risk. Snapshots: M0, M3, M6, M12.
---
CULTURE_M — Culture Change and Collaboration Reset
Catalog ID: `ONA-BMK-CULTURE-M-v1` · Default size: 900 nodes · Topology: `sbm` + communities of practice
Models a culture transformation programme with community-of-practice seeding, cross-functional collaboration reset, and hybrid working norm shifts. Snapshots: before, mid, after.
---
SUCCESSION_M — Leadership Succession and Key-Person Risk
Catalog ID: `ONA-BMK-SUCCESSION-M-v1` · Default size: 800 nodes · Topology: `ba` + `hier_corp`
A scale-free influence network with high broker concentration, designed for succession planning and key-person risk analysis. Supports node-removal simulations. Snapshots: baseline and removal simulation.

Synthetic Outcome Variables
Each generated dataset includes per-node synthetic outcome scores derived from network position and node attributes. These are not ground-truth labels — they are plausible benchmarks for testing dashboard rendering, prediction models, and outcome-to-structure correlation analyses.
Variable	Range	Driven by
`engagement_score`	0–1	Trust degree, closeness, structural constraint
`innovation_score`	0–1	Communication degree, betweenness, AI adoption score
`burnout_risk`	0–1	Communication overload, betweenness, low trust
`adoption_velocity`	0–1	AI_M scenario only — change readiness × AI adoption score

Visualiser Features
Canvas
Force-directed layout via D3 v7
Colour nodes by department, seniority, location, AI adoption, change readiness, or attrition risk
Continuous attributes use a sequential Viridis colour scale (perceptually uniform; dark = low, yellow = high)
Size nodes by degree (linear) or betweenness (log scale — prevents hub domination in scale-free networks)
Label selected nodes or all nodes
Zoom, pan, freeze/resume layout
Hover Interaction
Hovering a node highlights its ego network: direct neighbours light up with a cyan ring, non-neighbours fade to 20% opacity, incident edges brighten and thicken
Node tooltip: degree, betweenness, AI adoption, change readiness, attrition risk, and structural flags (articulation point, AI agent, adoption champion, key person)
Edge tooltip: layer, weight, and relational context (same department, same location, cross-legacy)
Highlight respects the current layer filter — hidden layers do not count as connections
Layer Filter Panel
Toggle individual layers on/off with a clearly sized eye button
Solo a single layer by clicking its row; click again to clear
Show all / Hide all / Clear solo header controls
Edge count in the footer and degree-distribution sparkline update live as layers are toggled
Layer percentages show each layer's share of the currently visible edge total
Metrics Footer
Node count · visible/total edge count · density · average degree · clustering coefficient · component count · giant component share · articulation point count · active layer count · degree distribution sparkline
Overlays
Validation — scenario-specific structural acceptance criteria checked after each generation
Outcomes — engagement, innovation, and burnout scores by department as bar charts; adoption velocity shown for AI_M
Reproducibility
Every network is fully reproducible from its seed pair. Same scenario + same topology seed + same attribute seed = same network, every time. The dual-seed design allows structural variation (`seed_topology`) while holding the employee population constant (`seed_attributes`), enabling controlled comparisons across topology families.

Export
File	Contents
`nodes.csv`	Full node attribute table including all generated scores
`edges.csv`	Full edge table with layer, weight, direction, same-dept, same-location, same-legacy
`metrics.json`	Network-level and per-group structural metrics

Validation
Every scenario includes acceptance criteria checked automatically after generation. A few examples:
DEMO_M — at least 2 visible silo clusters; at least 1 articulation point
AI_M — at least 3 AI-agent nodes; adoption champions in the top-betweenness decile
MA_M — cross-legacy tie ratio below 15% at M0
SUCCESSION_M — broker concentration above threshold; articulation points identified
Results are shown in the Validation overlay and included in the `platform_payload.json` export.
